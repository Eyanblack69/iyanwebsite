SMAW

Stick welding, or shielded metal arc welding (SMAW), is a type of welding in which the weld is laid using a consumable electrode that has been coated in flux. The electrode and the workpiece form an electric arc during SMAW, which produces extreme heat that melts the electrode and the base metal. In addition to stabilizing the arc and shielding the molten weld pool from airborne contaminants, the flux covering the electrode creates a slag layer that protects the weld during cooling. Because of its adaptability, capacity to weld a wide range of metals and thicknesses, and appropriateness for distant and outdoor welding situations where alternative techniques would not be feasible, SMAW welding is commonly utilized. It is frequently employed in industrial applications such as fabrication, maintenance, repair, and building.
