H.E

A multidisciplinary field of study, home economics covers a range of topics related to managing a house, raising a family, consumer education, nutrition, food preparation, textiles, apparel, housing, and personal finance. It seeks to provide people with the information and abilities required to successfully run their homes, make wise decisions as consumers, advance their health and wellbeing, and acquire useful life skills. A home economics education frequently combines academic information about child development, nutrition, budgeting, and household administration with practical skills like sewing and cooking. Through home economics, people can maintain visually beautiful and practical living spaces, design healthy and sustainable lifestyles, and take advantage of the opportunities and challenges that come with living in a family and a community .
